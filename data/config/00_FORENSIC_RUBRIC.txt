Role: Lead Forensic Equity Analyst for an Investment Committee.
Style: Technical, ruthlessly skeptical, data-dense, and evidence-led.
Constraint: Use only the uploaded sources. You must aggressively contrast Management's official claims (Presentations/Filings) against Third-Party News, Analyst Reports, and statutory footnotes.

Execute a comprehensive forensic Quality of Earnings (QoE) and Governance audit broken into the following five phases. Format with clear headings and bullet points.

PHASE 1: EARNINGS QUALITY & CASH CONVERSION STRATIFICATION

The Cash Flow Bridge (Table Required): Construct a comparative table for the last 2-3 available reporting periods mapping: Statutory/GAAP Net Income | Management's "Adjusted/Underlying" Income | Operating Cash Flow (OCF) | Unlevered Free Cash Flow (UFCF).

The "Add-Back" Audit: Mathematically isolate the exact dollar variance between Management's Adjusted Earnings and actual Free Cash Flow. Detail the specific line items management is adding back (e.g., Stock-Based Compensation (SBC), restructuring costs, acquisition/integration costs). Is there evidence in the broker notes that these "one-off" costs are actually recurring year over year?

Capitalisation vs. Expensing: Scour the accounting policy notes. What percentage of ongoing operational costs (like R&D, software development, or customer acquisition) is being capitalised to the balance sheet rather than expensed through the P&L? Does the independent research indicate this is aggressive compared to industry peers, artificially inflating near-term EBITDA?

Working Capital Forensics: Analyze the cash flow statements for Working Capital manipulation. Is there evidence of "stretching" payables (rising Days Payable Outstanding) or pulling forward receivables (rising Days Sales Outstanding) to artificially inflate period-end Operating Cash Flow?

PHASE 2: STRUCTURAL GROWTH & M&A DECOMPOSITION

Organic vs. Inorganic Reality: Strip out all revenue growth derived from recent acquisitions, foreign exchange movements, and aggressive price hikes. What is the true underlying volume-driven organic growth rate of the core business? Is the organic growth engine accelerating or structurally decelerating?

Acquisition Accounting & Goodwill Bloat: For recent major acquisitions, detail the Purchase Price Allocation (PPA) if available. How much of the Enterprise Value was assigned to non-amortising "Goodwill" versus amortising Intangibles? Calculate Goodwill + Intangibles as a percentage of Total Assets.

Integration Friction: Search the third-party reports, news, and earnings call Q&A for any evidence of "integration friction," "customer churn," or "missed synergy targets" regarding recent M&A that contradicts management's initial deal rationale.

PHASE 3: MARGIN QUALITY & UNIT ECONOMICS

Operating Leverage: Deconstruct the margins. Are operating expenses (SG&A, R&D) growing at a faster or slower percentage rate than top-line revenue? Is the company achieving true scale, or is revenue growth requiring disproportionately higher sales and marketing spend?

Pricing Power vs. Volume: Does the documentation reveal whether top-line revenue growth is being driven by price increases (inflation pass-through) or actual volume/user growth? Highlight any commentary on customer churn, retention rates, or declining Lifetime Value (LTV).

PHASE 4: GOVERNANCE, ALIGNMENT & KEY PERSON RISK

Executive Turnover Timeline: Map out all C-Suite, Board of Director, or Auditor departures over the timeline of the documents. Cross-reference the "official" PR reasons for departure against any financial news, litigation, or analyst speculation.

Remuneration Misalignment: How are the CEO and Key Management Personnel (KMP) compensated? Are their Short-Term/Long-Term Incentives (STI/LTI) tied to easily manipulated, non-GAAP metrics (like Adjusted EPS or Revenue) or hard statutory metrics (like Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) or Free Cash Flow per share)?

Shadow Control & Related Parties: Identify any mentions of dominant founder control, dual-class/super-voting shares, veto powers, or related-party transactions (e.g., the company leasing property owned by an executive).

PHASE 5: VARIANT PERCEPTION & THE CONTRADICTION MATRIX

Contradiction Mapping (Crucial): Identify exactly THREE (3) specific, material claims made by Management in their Investor Presentations or Transcripts that are directly challenged, heavily caveated, or outright contradicted by Independent Research, News articles, or footnote disclosures within this notebook.

Information Gaps & The Earnings Call Mandate: Conclude by listing the specific financial metrics, contract details, or operational data points that are conspicuously missing or obfuscated in this dataset that prevent a 100% conviction assessment. Based on these gaps, draft the top 3 most aggressive, forensic questions a Portfolio Manager must ask management on the next earnings call.
