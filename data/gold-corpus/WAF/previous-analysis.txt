## Evidence
- Kiaka SA ownership -- unresolved sovereign demand: Fact: In August 2025, the Burkina Faso government formally requested an additional 35% of Kiaka SA for paid consideration; WAF was suspended from ASX from 1 September 2025 and reinstated 25 November 2025; discussions remain active as at the December 2025 quarterly. Inference: if granted at full scope, WAF's economic interest in Kiaka SA would fall from 85% to approximately 50% of the subsidiary that holds the primary growth asset. (Source: WAF December 2025 Quarterly; WAF September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- 2024 Mining Code equity transfer -- complete: Fact: State equity in SOMISA (Sanbrado), Kiaka SA, and Toega SA increased from 10% to 15% in July-August 2025 for nil proceeds, reducing WAF's interest from 90% to 85%; A$33.4m reduction in retained profits recorded in H1 2025 consolidated statement. (Source: WAF H1 2025 Financial Report, Quality: )
- Royalty structure -- sliding scale, price-linked: Fact: Burkina Faso imposes a gross production royalty at 7% for gold US$2,000-3,000/oz, rising by 1% per US$500/oz increment above US$3,000/oz (reaching 12% for gold above US$5,500/oz); plus a 1% community development levy payable at all levels; effective April 2025, the base royalty rate increased by 1%; at WAF's Q4 2025 average realised price of US$4,058/oz, the applicable royalty rate was 10%. (Source: WAF December 2025 Quarterly; WAF H1 2025 Financial Report, Quality: )
- Priority dividends -- non-discretionary confirmed: Fact: The State's 15% equity in each subsidiary entitles it to an annual non-discretionary priority dividend equal to 15% of subsidiary profits under OHADA accounting principles; SOMISA paid A$99m to the State in Q3 2025 (15% of retained earnings to December 2024); WAF provisioned A$26.4m for the SOMISA H1 2025 priority dividend; Kiaka SA and Toega SA recorded no OHADA profit in H1 2025 so no provision was required for those entities. (Source: WAF H1 2025 Financial Report; WAF September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Balance sheet -- net cash by December 2025: Fact: Net debt of A$132.8m at 30 June 2025 transitioned to notional net cash of US$33m (approximately A$51m) by 30 September 2025 and US$210m (approximately A$323m at 0.65 AUD/USD) by 31 December 2025; gross cash A$584m with A$177m unsold gold bullion at year-end; borrowings were secured facilities of US$265m combined limit from Sprott Resource Lending Corp and Coris Bank International, fully drawn at 30 June 2025; A$28m principal repaid in Q4 2025. (Source: WAF December 2025 Quarterly; WAF September 2025 Quarterly; WAF H1 2025 Financial Report, Quality: )
- FY2025 production and cost -- guidance met fifth consecutive year: Fact: Group produced 300,383 oz (guidance 290,000-360,000 oz); group AISC US$1,488/oz (A$2,308/oz at 0.6448 average FX); average realised price US$3,525/oz (A$5,466/oz); Q4 operating cash flow A$389m after A$48m income tax; Q4 group AISC US$1,561/oz driven by sliding-scale royalty applied to US$4,058/oz average realised price. (Source: WAF December 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Kiaka ramp-up cost trajectory: Fact: Kiaka site sustaining cost fell from US$1,921/oz (Q3 2025, August-September only) to US$1,649/oz (Q4 2025, first full quarter); grid power contributed in December 2025, enabling higher throughput and treatment of harder, higher-grade ore; Kiaka 10-year target is 248,000 oz/year from 2026. Inference: Q4 is not steady-state; further cost improvement expected as full grid power replaces backup diesel generators and ore blend improves. (Source: WAF December 2025 Quarterly; WAF September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- 10-year production target and mineral inventory: Fact: Ore Reserves 6.5 Moz gold and Mineral Resources 12.2 Moz gold (both updated August 2025); 10-year production target averages 480,000 oz/year from 2025 to 2034, peaking at 569,000 oz in 2029; Sanbrado mine plan extended to 2035; Kiaka 248,000 oz/year target from 2026; 2026 annual guidance not yet released (planned Q1 2026). Reserve grade in g/t not stated in corpus. (Source: WAF H1 2025 Financial Report; WAF September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
