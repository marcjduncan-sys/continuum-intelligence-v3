## Evidence
- FY26 production guidance -- revised down twice: Fact: NST's FY26 group production guidance was originally set at 1,700-1,850 koz; revised to 1,600-1,700 koz as at the December 2025 quarterly. Inference: two downward revisions within one financial year indicate above-expectation operational disruption during the KCGM expansion period. (Source: NST Q2 FY26 Quarterly (December 2025), Quality: )
- AISC guidance -- revised up, elevated for FY26: Fact: FY26 AISC guidance midpoint is approximately A$2,700-2,800/oz; the expansion at KCGM is the primary driver of the elevated cost in FY26 as sustaining and growth capital is at peak during construction. At spot gold above A$4,000/oz, the margin remains material but is compressed relative to a normalised AISC profile. (Source: NST Q2 FY26 Quarterly (December 2025); NST H1 FY26 Half Year Report, Quality: )
- KCGM mill expansion -- commissioning underway: Fact: The KCGM mill expansion at Kalgoorlie is NST's flagship capital project, targeting 26 Mtpa throughput. Commissioning is in progress as at the December 2025 quarterly. Inference: the cost and production disruption in FY26 is attributable to construction; the bull thesis depends on throughput confirmation post-commissioning. (Source: NST Q2 FY26 Quarterly (December 2025); NST H1 FY26 Half Year Report, Quality: )
- Balance sheet -- net cash position maintained: Fact: NST held net cash of A$293m at December 2025. No significant debt maturity pressure disclosed in the corpus. Funding runway is adequate through the KCGM expansion without requiring equity issuance at current gold prices. (Source: NST Q2 FY26 Quarterly (December 2025), Quality: )
- AISC margin at spot and stress case: Fact: At FY26 AISC guidance midpoint of A$2,750/oz and spot gold above A$4,000/oz, the margin is approximately A$1,250/oz. At a stress-case gold price of A$3,500/oz (approximately US$2,200/oz at 0.63), the margin narrows to approximately A$750/oz -- still positive but cash generation is materially reduced. At A$2,200/oz spot equivalent, NST is below breakeven on an AISC basis. (Source: NST Q2 FY26 Quarterly (December 2025) -- inference applied to guidance figures, Quality: )
- Yandal -- cost drag on group AISC: Fact: The Yandal operations (Jundee and Thunderbox) carry a higher per-ounce cost than KCGM due to lower grade and scale. The corpus confirms Yandal's contribution to the elevated group AISC in FY26. Inference: Yandal is a structural drag that persists beyond the KCGM expansion; no asset disposal or strategy change is disclosed. (Source: NST H1 FY26 Half Year Report; NST FY25 Annual Report, Quality: )
- Pogo (Alaska) -- jurisdiction and FX exposure: Fact: NST owns and operates the Pogo underground mine in Alaska, USA. This is the sole non-Australian asset in the portfolio, introducing USD revenue and cost exposure plus US mining jurisdiction risk. Pogo's contribution to group production and AISC is not separately quantified in the corpus query responses. (Source: NST FY25 Annual Report, Quality: )
- Ore Reserve life: Fact: NST's disclosed Ore Reserve life is approximately 13 years, supporting the long-term production profile. Reserve grade of approximately 1.5 g/t is the corpus estimate; the precise JORC breakdown (Measured/Indicated/Inferred) by asset is not separately enumerated in the corpus query responses. (Source: NST FY25 Annual Report -- inference from reserve life disclosure, Quality: )
