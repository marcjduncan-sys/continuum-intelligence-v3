## Evidence
- Net debt trajectory -- accelerating deleveraging: Fact: Net debt A$849m at 30 June 2025 (gearing 15%), reduced to A$659m at 30 September 2025 (gearing 11%) after A$170m in early debt repayments. A further A$110m repaid subsequent to quarter end. No debt repayments due until FY29. Total liquidity A$1.3bn including A$525m undrawn revolving credit facility extended to 2028. Remaining debt is US Private Placements maturing FY29, FY31, FY32, FY34, and FY36. (Source: EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Copper by-product credits -- structural cost advantage: Fact: Ernest Henry FY25 AISC A$(2,376)/oz; Q1 FY26 A$(1,926)/oz. Northparkes FY25 AISC A$(2,514)/oz; Q1 FY26 A$(2,993)/oz. Copper credits make approximately 20% of group gold production effectively cost-free on an AISC basis. Ernest Henry copper production 47,776t in FY25; Northparkes 28,485t. Combined, these two assets generated A$1,486m revenue in FY25. (Source: EVN FY25 Annual Report, EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Group AISC FY25 and Q1 FY26 trajectory: Fact: Group AISC A$1,653/oz in FY25 (continuing operations). Q1 FY26 group AISC A$1,724/oz (continuing operations, ex Mt Rawdon which ceased active mining). Company confirmed on track to deliver FY26 production and cost guidance. FY26 group production guidance koz range not explicitly stated in corpus. (Source: EVN FY25 Annual Report, EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Red Lake -- persistent cost drag, improving cash generation: Fact: Red Lake FY25 AISC A$2,726/oz; Q1 FY26 A$2,703/oz. Gold production 127,632oz in FY25 (approximately 17% of group). Q1 FY26 record net mine cash flow A$39m (second consecutive record). Strategy: margin over volume -- gold grade mined increased 24% to 5.15 g/t in Q1 FY26. Non-current assets in Canada A$1,795.5m (22% of group total). AUD:CAD 5% FX move impacts net assets by A$182.1m. (Source: EVN FY25 Annual Report, EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Mungari mill expansion -- delivered ahead of schedule, under budget: Fact: Mungari 4.2 mill expansion completed commissioning October 2025, nine months ahead of schedule. Final cost A$212m, 15% below original budget. Processing throughput increased 48% to 919kt in Q1 FY26. AISC stepped down from A$2,753/oz (FY25) to A$1,982/oz (Q1 FY26). Castle Hill open pit provides baseload ore feed for at least 8-10 years. (Source: EVN FY25 Annual Report, EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Cowal -- highest cash contributor, entering OPC waste stripping phase: Fact: Cowal produced 330,008oz in FY25 (44% of group) with FY25 AISC A$1,752/oz, rising to A$2,314/oz in Q1 FY26. Cost increase driven by planned bi-annual maintenance, weather disruptions, and open pit Stage H completion before waste stripping for Stage I. Cowal accounted for approximately one-third of group operating mine cash flow (A$215m) in Q1 FY26. Board-approved OPC extends open pit life to 2042 at A$430m capex over 7 years. (Source: EVN FY25 Annual Report, EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
- Cash flow upside at current spot prices: Fact: At current spot gold and copper prices (basis Q1 FY26 guidance), estimated operating mine cash flow increases from A$2,755m to A$3,325m (A$570m upside), and mine cash flow before major capital from A$2,515m to A$3,085m. Q1 FY26 net mine cash flow A$366m was a record, nearly 20% above the prior record. Every operation delivered positive net mine cash flow. (Source: EVN September 2025 Quarterly, Quality: )
