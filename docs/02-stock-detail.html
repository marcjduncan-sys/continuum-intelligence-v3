Bottom Line Up Front

BHP presents a moderate upside skew at current prices, with the Evidence Weighted Price of A$61.20 implying a +16.4% gap to the live market price of A$52.56. The ACH framework maps four competing cases across 10 evidence domains. The Bull Case is supported by iron ore volume recovery and copper optionality; the Base Case reflects a moderate China stimulus recovery with disciplined capital allocation; the Bear Case is driven by iron ore structural headwinds and Jansen capital risk; the Swing Case is the US tariff escalation, which could materially shift the thesis in either direction depending on China's policy response. Evidence weight currently favours the Base and Bull cases at a combined 65% probability, producing the upside skew.
