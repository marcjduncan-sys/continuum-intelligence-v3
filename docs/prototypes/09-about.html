The Problem

Why the existing tools fail the best investors

The top 1% of institutional investors — portfolio managers running A$100M+ mandates, senior analysts at tier-one funds — have access to every terminal, every data feed, and every broker note. And yet, the core analytical process has not changed in 40 years. The sell-side produces a price target. The buy-side either agrees or disagrees. The evidence that drove the conclusion is buried in footnotes, if it appears at all.

The result is a process that is opinion-heavy and evidence-light. Price targets are anchored to the analyst's prior view. Confidence intervals are invisible. The Bear Case is always a footnote, never a first-class hypothesis. And when the evidence changes, the price target rarely does — until it is too late.

Continuum Intelligence was built to fix this. Not to add another data feed. Not to add another AI chatbot. To replace the analytical process itself.

The Methodology

Analysis of Competing Hypotheses — adapted for institutional investment

The ACH methodology was developed by the US intelligence community as a structured analytical technique for evaluating competing hypotheses against a body of evidence. We adapted it for institutional investment research. The result is a framework that forces the analyst to consider all four scenarios — not just the one they already believe — and to weight each one by the evidence, not by conviction.

01
Four Named Cases
Every stock thesis is structured around four competing hypotheses: the Bull Case, the Base Case, the Bear Case, and the Swing Case. Each case is a first-class hypothesis with its own price target, probability weight, and evidence mapping. The Swing Case captures the asymmetric wildcard — the single factor that could resolve the thesis materially in either direction.
02
Evidence For / Evidence Against
Every piece of evidence — ASX announcements, macro data, quantitative signals, management commentary, industry news — is classified as supporting or contradicting each case. The balance of evidence across all four cases determines the probability weight assigned to each. This is not a model. It is a structured evidence audit.
03
The Evidence Weighted Price
The EWP is the platform's core proprietary output. It is calculated as the probability-weighted average of the four case price targets: EWP = (Bull × weight) + (Base × weight) + (Bear × weight) + (Swing × weight). It is not a price target. It is a probability-weighted price derived from the evidence balance. It updates dynamically as new evidence enters the system.
04
The EWP Gap
The gap between the live market price and the EWP is the platform's primary decision signal. A positive EWP gap indicates the evidence supports a higher price than the market is currently pricing. A negative EWP gap indicates the evidence supports a lower price. This is the most powerful single output on the platform — and it sits front and centre on every stock page.
Design Principles

What we believe about how the best investors work

⚖
Evidence over opinion
Every output on the platform is derived from evidence, not from the analyst's prior view. The EWP is calculated from the evidence balance, not from a DCF model built backward from a conclusion. If the evidence changes, the EWP changes. Automatically.
🔍
Transparency over black boxes
The EWP derivation is fully auditable. Every case weight, every price target, every evidence item is visible. There are no hidden models. The evidence-to-price chain is transparent by design. If you cannot explain why the EWP is what it is, the platform has failed.
⚡
Speed over comprehensiveness
The Home page is designed to answer "what needs my attention?" in under 5 seconds. The Research page is designed to answer "what is the evidence gap?" in under 30 seconds. Information density is a feature, not a bug. We do not use whitespace to make the platform feel premium — we use precision.
🤝
Intelligence that compounds
The AI agents — Analyst, Portfolio Manager, Strategist — are not chatbots. They are specialised intelligence agents that understand the ACH framework, the evidence dossier, and the portfolio context. Every response is grounded in evidence and cited back to the specific data point. The more you use the platform, the more the intelligence compounds.
