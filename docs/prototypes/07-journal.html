Tariff announcement activates Swing Case — PBOC response is the key variable
4 Apr 2026 · 09:18 AEST
EvidenceMacro

The overnight US tariff announcement (34% on Chinese imports) is the most significant macro event for the BHP thesis since the PBOC rate cut in November. The key question is not whether this is bad for iron ore demand — it clearly is — but whether the PBOC responds with a stimulus package large enough to offset the demand destruction.

I have updated the Swing Case weight from 8% to 10% and the Bear Case from 22% to 25%. The EWP has moved from A$61.80 to A$61.20. The upside skew is maintained, but the conviction has narrowed. The next material evidence event is the PBOC press conference scheduled for 7 April.

Key tripwire: If the PBOC announces infrastructure spending above RMB 500bn, the Swing Case resolves bullishly and the Bull Case weight should be increased. If no stimulus is announced within 2 weeks, the Bear Case weight should increase to 30%+.

FMG
FMG Bear Case thesis — structural iron ore decline is the base scenario, not the bear
2 Apr 2026 · 14:30 AEST
ThesisRisk

I have been reviewing the FMG thesis and I am increasingly concerned that what we are calling the "Bear Case" is actually the most likely scenario. The Bear Case holds 40% probability weight — the highest of any case. The Base Case at 35% is barely more likely than the Bear Case.

The structural argument is compelling: Simandou Phase 1 adds 60Mt of high-grade iron ore to a market that is already in surplus. FMG's ore quality disadvantage (62% vs 65% benchmark) means it will be the marginal producer in a price decline. There is no copper optionality to offset this. There is no Jansen-style long-term growth story.

Decision: I am recommending a reduction to 2–3% portfolio weight, pending the PM's rebalancing model. The EWP gap of −15.1% is the clearest sell signal in the current portfolio.

FMG · Thesis · Risk · EWP: A$15.80

GMG hyperscaler pre-commitments — Bull Case weight increased to 30%
18 Mar 2026 · 11:45 AEST
EvidenceDecision

GMG announced pre-commitment agreements with two hyperscalers for data centre development in Sydney and Melbourne, totalling 420MW of capacity. This is a material confirmation of the Bull Case thesis — that GMG's data centre pipeline is being absorbed faster than the market is pricing.
