01 / Thesis

The decision is straightforward: BHP works if copper keeps taking mix while iron ore remains good enough to fund returns.

This is a refinement of the original research page language, not a new workstation shell.
Decision-first
At A$52.56, the market is paying for durable cash flow but not fully for a higher-quality commodity mix. The base case only needs three things to remain true: iron ore above roughly US$90/t, FY26 copper guidance of 1.9-2.0 Mt stays credible, and management keeps allocating capital inside the strong balance sheet plus minimum 50% payout framework. If those hold, EWP of A$56.55 is reasonable.

What changed in the story is earnings mix. Copper generated the largest share of H1 FY26 underlying EBITDA, which matters because copper deserves a higher strategic multiple than bulk commodities when supply is structurally tight. BHP also retains visible copper growth options at Escondida, South Australia, Vicuña and Resolution, while iron ore still funds the balance sheet and dividend base.

What changes my mind
Iron ore below the mid-US$80s/t, a copper delivery miss, or capital intensity rising without a clear return uplift. Any of those would compress the EWP quickly.

07 / Deep research

The case is strongest when read as a portfolio of cash engines rather than a single-commodity view.

The stock’s resilience still comes from iron ore. Its rerating potential increasingly comes from copper.
Expandable

BHP’s current valuation is still anchored by iron ore economics, but the forward narrative is increasingly determined by copper. Iron ore still provides the scale, margin and cash-return base, while copper now provides the cleaner structural growth angle. That mix reduces the chance the market can continue to price BHP as merely a China steel-cycle expression.

The critical operating asset is Escondida. The raised FY26 production range supports the Base case, but it also makes Escondida the first place to look for disappointment if the thesis starts to weaken. The market will forgive modest capex if copper execution remains strong. It will not forgive cost inflation plus weak grade delivery.

China remains the largest external variable. A PMI above 50 is supportive, but it is not the same as a forceful demand impulse. The EWP therefore works only while the copper uplift is large enough to offset a middling rather than exuberant China backdrop.
