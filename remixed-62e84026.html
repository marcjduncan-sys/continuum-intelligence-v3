Continuum Intelligence Platform

Analytical Pipeline

Twelve steps from ticker symbol to investment page. Each specialist builds one layer of understanding. The master agent accumulates context progressively. The user sees only the output.

Phase 1 Evidence Assembly

Seven specialists analyse one dimension each. The master agent reads each output and builds a progressively richer picture.

01
Industry Structure Analyst

What kind of industry is this? Are the structural conditions that prevailed in the past likely to persist?

Receives
Sector classification, industry data
Produces
Industry structural map — concentration, barriers, lifecycle position, pressure points
02
Competitive Positioning Analyst

What is the company's position within that industry? How durable are its advantages?

Receives
Industry map + company disclosures, market share data, competitive intelligence
Produces
Position assessment — WIDENING / STABLE / NARROWING — with advantage durability map
03
Governance & Management Analyst

Who controls the company and what incentivises them? Will they preserve or erode the moat?

Receives
Prior context + board composition, executive contracts, remuneration, insider ownership
Produces
Governance assessment — accountability rated STRUCTURAL / NOMINAL / ABSENT
04
Accounting & Forensics Analyst

Can we trust the numbers? Where might reported earnings diverge from economic reality?

Receives
Prior context + 10-year financials, auditor reports, key audit matters
Produces
Earnings quality — HIGH / MODERATE / LOW — with red flags and quantified impact
05
Forward Guidance Analyst

What is management promising, and is it credible given the evidence?

Receives
Prior context + management guidance, consensus estimates, strategic announcements
Produces
Guidance credibility assessment, strategic event flags, say-do gap analysis
06
Financial History Analyst

What does the 10-year financial record show — in the context of everything above?

Receives
All prior context + full financial history, Piotroski F-Score, Altman Z, DuPont decomposition
Produces
Financial trajectory in full context — where data confirms or contradicts the thesis
07
Belief Trajectory Analyst

What has the market believed, how have those beliefs shifted, and what does it believe now?

Receives
Technical analysis + valuation history — multiples, filing windows, EPS surprise, AAR
Produces
Belief shift narrative — implied scenario from current multiple vs historical range
Phase 2 Synthesis & Valuation

The master agent constructs competing scenarios. Specialist agents value each one. A final agent assigns probability weights.

08
Competing Hypotheses — Master Agent

What are the 3–5 competing futures? Which survives disconfirmation best?

Receives
All seven specialist outputs from Phase 1, accumulated in context
Produces
Scenarios with survival scores, evidence mapping, linchpins, tripwires. No prices yet.
09
Forecast Agent ×4 scenarios

If this scenario is true, what do the next three years look like?

Receives
Full context + one scenario's specific conditions from Step 8
Produces
3-year earnings path — Revenue → EBITDA → Net Income → Free Cash Flow
10
Multiple Agent ×4 scenarios

Given this scenario's earnings, what multiple does the market assign?

Receives
Step 9 earnings path + Step 7 valuation history + peer multiples
Produces
Selected multiple with rationale → implied share price per scenario
11
Weight Assignment Agent

What probability does each scenario carry — and where does the market disagree?

Receives
Survival scores + implied prices + current market price
Produces
Probability weights, market-implied vs evidence-adjusted, skew classification
All outputs converge
Phase 3 Front End Assembly

One agent transforms all upstream outputs into the page JSON. It generates no new analysis.

12
Front End Population Agent

Transform twelve steps of analysis into the five things the user actually needs to see.

Receives
All of Steps 1–11 — scenarios, forecasts, multiples, weights, evidence
Produces
Complete page JSON — every field populated, every number traceable to its source

What the User Sees

The user sees none of this machinery. They see a page with five sections above the fold.

01
What the Price Embeds
One sentence describing the market's current belief about this company.
02
Four Scenarios
Each with a label, probability weight, direction arrow, and implied price.
03
Risk Structure
Why the upside is harder to achieve than the downside — or vice versa.
04
What to Do
Wait, or act — and what changes at the next decision point.
05
When It Resolves
The specific events and dates that will shift the scenario weights.
Below the fold: the full evidence base for anyone who wants to drill in.
